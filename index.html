Chanaka Bandara

Ph.D. Student at the University of Delaware

I am a Ph.D. student in the Center for Autonomous and Robotics Systems (ICARS) at the University of Delaware. I am advised by Dr. Bert Tanner and co-advised by Dr. Guoquan (Paul) Huang. Additionally, I am a Collaborative Researcher at the Multimedia Information Analysis Laboratory (MIA Lab) in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Kentucky, working with Dr. Sen-ching Samson Cheung.

I work at the intersection of Computer Vision, Generative AI, Deep Learning, and Robotics:

  1. Training-Free Style Transfer & Diffusion Models: Developing novel training-free style transfer pipelines using SDXL and IP-Adapter with tri-guided conditioning (style, content, text) achieving controllable stylization with strong content preservation at high resolution on single GPU.
  2. AI in Medicine & Active Learning: Creating active learning frameworks that combine self-supervised learning with adaptive clustering techniques to achieve near full-supervision performance on medical image segmentation with minimal labeled data.
  3. Underwater Robotics & State Estimation: Developing robust hybrid state estimators for autonomous underwater vehicles that integrate dynamic and kinematic models, with successful validation on Aqua2 AUV in challenging underwater environments.
  4. Swarm Robotics & Cooperative Control: Developing coordination strategies for marine surface vehicles using reinforcement learning (PPO) and navigation functions for autonomous navigation, and cooperative Kalman filtering for multi-robot sensor fusion to enable autonomous water quality monitoring using Jaiabot micro-AUVs.

Prior to joining UD, I received my Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Peradeniya, Sri Lanka. At UoP, I worked with Dr. Sanjeeva Maithripala, Dr. Asanga Ratnaweera, Dr. Prasanna Gunawardane, and Dr. Maheshi Dissanayake.
