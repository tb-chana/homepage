Chanaka Bandara

Ph.D. Student at the University of Delaware | Machine Learning and Robotics

I am a Ph.D. student at the University of Delaware, working at the intersection of Computer Vision, Generative AI, and Robotics. I am advised by Dr. Bert Tanner and co-advised by Dr. Guoquan (Paul) Huang. I am also a Collaborative Researcher at the Multimedia Information Analysis Laboratory (MIA Lab) at the University of Kentucky, working with Dr. Sen-ching Samson Cheung.

My research lies at the intersection of Computer Vision, Generative AI, and Robotics, with a focus on diffusion models, active learning, and learning-based control:

  1. Generative Diffusion-Based Style Transfer: Developing structure-preserving diffusion methods using implicit neural style fields, pure additive prior injection, and regularized latent guidance to enable fine-grained appearance control from pure noise while preserving content structure.
  2. Diffusion as Implicit Optimization: Interpreting reverse diffusion as an implicit optimization process and designing adaptive timestep and correction strategies for efficient, stable task-conditioned generation.
  3. AI in Medicine & Active Learning: Building task-aligned active learning frameworks that combine self-supervised representations with adaptive GMM-based clustering to achieve near fully supervised medical image segmentation with reduced annotation cost.
  4. Reinforcement Learning & Probabilistic State Estimation: Learning-based control and estimation methods that integrate reinforcement learning, analytical dynamics, and probabilistic sensor fusion for robust decision-making under partial observability.
  5. Multi-Agent Systems & Cooperative Control: Optimization-based coordination of multi-robot systems using navigation functions and cooperative Kalman filtering for distributed sensing and control.

Prior to joining UD, I received my Bachelor's degree in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Peradeniya, Sri Lanka. At UoP, I worked with Dr. Maheshi Dissanayake, Dr. Sanjeeva Maithripala, Dr. Asanga Ratnaweera, and Dr. Prasanna Gunawardane.
